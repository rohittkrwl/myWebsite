  • My Cart: May’21-May’21
    • Modified a shopping website as part of my training at Infosys.
    • Used Spring boot and Angular 10 to add a component Card designed to enable users to add credit and debit cards which can be used for payment purpose.
    • The front-end of the application was designed using Angular 10. It was designed to consume APIs received from back-end.
    • Reactive forms and functions were used to make the experience smooth helping the customer to add, delete and update cards. The data was synced between both ends of the application and any data altered was being reflected into the database in tandem. Prompts were also used to avoid customers from making errors and avoiding alteration of data due to hasty clicks.
    • Backend of the application was built using Spring-boot wherein separate Spring REST APIs were designed for each operation. All the connections with the database were made in backend.
    • Test cases were also built using JUnit Test and Mockito for verification of backend functionality.
    • CI/CD deployment tool Jenkins was used to build separate pipelines for both backend and frontend part of application to trigger automatic build and deployment.
    • Third party apps like Eclipse, Microsoft VS Code, Postman, and Jenkins were used for designing the website and verifications of functionality. Proper coding norms were followed and checked using PMD tool.

Academic Projects

  • VSSUT Satellite Launch Vehicle(VSLV): Oct’17-Present
    • Honoured as India’s first indigenously built student rocketry mission, we have made it to the Limca Book Of Records and India Book Of Records.
    • Acknowledged and honoured by ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) which has led to and MoU between VSSUT and ISRO and the latter setting up the country's first and only Space Innovation Centre.
    • We were awarded a Prize at Aerospace Expo in World Space Week and also won the Champions trophy and the Best Innovation Award of the Asia's 1st Intercollege Rocket Competition, organized by BRICS (Brazil, Russia, China, India, South Africa) & Space Development Nexus.
    • The aim is to launch a cansat over the dam to a height of 40km from where we would be able to measure the siltation levels by image sensing aFnd 3D mapping so that further measures can be taken by the government at the right time. We have done multiple successful launches leading to the same.
  • Air Purity Monitoring System(ARIA) : Dec’17-Jan’18
    • Built with an idea ‘Breathe safer, Stay safer‘.A low cost air monitoring device to measure impurity levels in the surrounding atmosphere to be established at houses, hospitals, and other public places.
    • A live saving device for the sewers who work in closed drains, and die due to high levels of methane and other poisonous gases, who can be alarmed beforehand in cases of hazard.
    • This idea was socially recognised and also appreciated by Padma Shri awardee Prof. Anil Kumar Gupta. The prototype has been successfully field-tested.
  • Bluetooth Controlled Bot : Mar’18-Apr’18
    • A totally wireless system consisting of a bot controlled by an app which controls the bot through a bluetooth module.
    • The bot was enabled with obstacle detection capability using sensors so it can stop and change the path in case any object comes in its way.
  • Soil Moisture Testing Device : Apr’18-May’18
    • To measure the soil moisture using sensor. The device is capable of automatically turning on the water in case the soil moisture turns low and also generates a warning in case the soil moisture is high. It basically ensures the supply of right amount of water for the growth of plants.
    • Can be implemented in small-scale in pot plants as well as can be modified for large-scale applictions in large fields to protect crops.
